# Start and Grow Your AI Advisory Business
### A practical, sequenced playbook — from first client to recurring revenue

*Prepared for Balamurugan Balakreshnan · June 2026*

---

## The arc at a glance

> **Niche → Proof → Recurring revenue → Educate → Scale**

Get one paying pilot with a documented result, and everything else compounds from there.

---

## Where to Start — The First 90 Days

### 1. Pick a sharp niche before anything else
Don't launch as a generic "AI consultant." Combine an **industry you already understand** with a **specific problem you solve** — e.g., *"AI workflow automation for accounting firms"* or *"AI customer support for home-services businesses."* Your prior-industry credibility is your single biggest unfair advantage and shortcuts the trust-building that normally takes years.

### 2. Define just 2–3 service offers + pricing
Keep it simple to sell:

| Offer | Format | Role in your funnel |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Readiness Assessment** | Paid, fixed fee | Foot-in-the-door product |
| **Implementation / quick-win pilot** | Project fee | Proves value, builds case study |
| **Managed AI services** | Monthly retainer | Recurring revenue engine |

Lead with the **paid assessment** → convert to **implementation** → graduate into a **retainer**.

### 3. Land 1–3 pilot clients fast — even at a discount
This is the most important early move. Your former employer, a contact's business, or a local SMB all work. The goal isn't profit yet; it's **proof**. Run an assessment + one quick win — start internal (meeting notes, invoice processing, email triage): low risk, easy to measure.

### 4. Turn results into case studies with hard numbers
*"Saved 30 hours/month, cut response time 60%, $1,400/month saved."* **Case studies beat certifications** when landing real clients. Document the baseline **before** you start so you can prove the delta.

### 5. Set up a lightweight presence
A clean LinkedIn profile, a one-page service/pricing sheet, and a repeatable assessment template (see companion file). You don't need a fancy website to win your first clients — you need **proof and a network**.

## How to Grow — Months 4–24

### 1. Build the recurring-revenue layer
Project work pays the bills; **managed services build the business.** Every implementation should end with an offer: *"Want me to monitor, optimize, and expand this monthly?"* Retainers smooth cash flow and reduce dependence on any single client.

### 2. Educate to sell (your #1 acquisition channel)
SMBs *know* they need AI but don't know where to start — so teaching **is** selling. Run free *"AI for [your industry]"* workshops, webinars, and lunch-and-learns, and publish short practical content. Authentic, human content earns trust — roughly **75% of buyers gut-check claims with people they trust** before buying.

### 3. Lean on your network and referrals
For SMBs, referrals and professional networks remain the dominant growth driver. Ask every happy pilot client for **one introduction**. Join SMB associations and local business groups.

### 4. Productize your methodology
Your repeatable engagement model **is** your product:

> **Assess → Identify highest-ROI use case → Pilot → Measure → Expand → Manage**

Templatize assessments, prompt libraries, and playbooks so delivery gets faster and margins improve.

### 5. Then scale capacity and scope
- **People:** solo → contractors/partners → small team as retainers stack up.
- **Scope:** add complementary offers — data cleanup, custom automations, industry-specific templates, team AI-literacy training.
- **Discipline:** adopt light **FinOps** (cost visibility on AI/cloud spend). Credible advisors keep clients' bills predictable, and security/compliance is increasingly the deciding factor in who SMBs trust.

---

## The Honest Guardrails (they protect your reputation)
- **Fix data before automating** — messy data, not bad AI, sinks most projects.
- **Target high-value processes deliberately** — AI helps some tasks and *degrades* others; "AI everywhere" fails.
- **Set realistic expectations** — sell 10–25% efficiency gains that compound, not overnight 10x miracles. Underpromise, overdeliver, and the case studies write themselves.

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | 1–4 | Pick niche; define 2–3 offers & pricing; set up LinkedIn + one-page service sheet; finalize your assessment template. |

| **Credibility** | 9–12 | Publish 1–2 case studies; start educational content/workshops; convert a pilot into a monthly retainer. |
