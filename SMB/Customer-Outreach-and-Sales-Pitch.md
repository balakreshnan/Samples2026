# Customer Outreach & Sales Pitch
### How to reach prospects and pitch your AI advisory services

*Prepared for Balamurugan Balakreshnan · June 2026*

> **Core play:** Outreach drives the **free/low-cost assessment** → the assessment surfaces the **paid pilot** → the pilot becomes a **case study + retainer.** Lead with value, never a hard sell.

---

## Part A — How to Reach Out to Customers

For SMB advisory work, **warm and educational channels beat cold volume.** Prioritize in this order.

### 1. Your existing network first (highest conversion)
Former colleagues, your prior industry's contacts, vendors — anyone who already trusts you. This is your fastest path to the first paid pilot. Make a list of **20–30 names** and reach out personally to each.

### 2. Referrals (compounding engine)
Every happy pilot client → ask for **one** introduction:
> *"Who else do you know running a [industry] business that's drowning in [the problem we just fixed]?"*

### 3. Educate-to-sell (scalable top-of-funnel)
SMBs know they need AI but don't know where to start — so teaching **is** selling.
- Free *"AI for [industry]"* workshops and lunch-and-learns
- A short LinkedIn post 2–3×/week sharing a real result
- A downloadable **"AI Readiness Checklist"** (your assessment template as a lead magnet)

### 4. Local + niche communities
Chambers of commerce, industry associations, referral groups (e.g., BNI), and trade meetups — wherever your specific niche actually gathers.

### 5. Targeted outreach last
Once your niche is sharp, send a **small batch of personalized** emails/LinkedIn messages to look-alikes of your pilot clients. Quality over volume.

### A simple 5-touch sequence (per prospect)

| Touch | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | LinkedIn connect + 1-line note | Get on their radar |
| 2 | Value post / share a result | Build credibility passively |
| 3 | Personalized email or DM | Offer the free assessment/workshop |
| 4 | Follow-up with a relevant case study | Re-engage with proof |
| 5 | Soft "close the loop" message | *"Worth a 20-min chat?"* |

> Lead with the **free/low-cost assessment**, not a hard sell. It's the easiest "yes" and converts naturally into paid work.

## Part B — The Pitch

### 🎯 Elevator pitch (15 seconds)
> *"I help [industry] businesses use AI to save hours every week on the repetitive work — without needing a tech team or a big budget. We start with a quick assessment that finds your single highest-payback opportunity, prove it with a small pilot, then expand from there."*

### 📋 The core sales narrative (use in any meeting)

1. **Hook the pain** — *"Most [industry] owners I talk to are losing 15–20 hours a week to repetitive admin — invoicing, email, scheduling, answering the same questions."*
2. **Reframe the fear** — *"The barrier isn't the tools anymore. They're cheap and don't need coding. The hard part is knowing where to start — 80% of failed AI projects fail on bad data or unclear goals, not the AI."*
3. **Position your method** — *"So I don't sell 'AI everywhere.' We run a 1-hour Readiness Assessment, find the one workflow with the highest payback, and pilot it in 4–8 weeks with off-the-shelf tools."*
4. **Quantify the outcome** — *"Clients typically reclaim 5–7 hours per week per person and $500–2,000/month, with a human always reviewing the output."*
5. **De-risk the ask** — *"The assessment is low-cost and you keep the findings whether or not we work together. Want to start there?"*

### ✉️ Ready-to-use templates

**Cold / warm email**
> **Subject:** A quick way to get 5+ hours/week back at [Company]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I work with [industry] businesses to put AI to work on the repetitive tasks that eat your team's week — invoicing, scheduling, email, customer FAQs — without a tech team or big spend.
>
> I don't pitch "AI everywhere." I run a short Readiness Assessment that pinpoints the *one* workflow with the highest payback, then we pilot it in a few weeks and measure the result.
>
> [Recent proof: *"A [similar business] reclaimed ~30 hours/month after our first pilot."*]
>
> Open to a 20-minute call to see if there's an obvious quick win for [Company]? You keep the assessment findings either way.
>
> — [Your name]

**LinkedIn connection note**
> Hi [Name] — I help [industry] businesses use AI to cut hours of repetitive admin each week. Sharing practical, no-hype results here. Would love to connect.

**Referral ask (to a happy client)**
> Glad the [workflow] pilot is paying off! Quick favor — who else do you know running a [industry] business that's buried in [the same problem]? Happy to give them the same free assessment.

**Workshop / lunch-and-learn invite**
> Hosting a free 45-min session: *"3 AI quick wins for [industry] businesses — and the one mistake that wastes money."* No pitch, just practical takeaways + a readiness checklist to take home. [Date / link]

### 💡 Objection one-liners

| Objection | Your response |
|---|---|
| *"We're too small / not technical."* | "That's exactly who this works best for — the tools need no coding, and we start with one small task." |
| *"Is our data ready?"* | "We check that first. If it's not, fixing it is step one — and that alone pays off." |
| *"Will it replace my staff?"* | "It replaces tasks, not people — it gives your team their time back for higher-value work." |
| *"How do I know it'll work?"* | "You don't have to guess — we pilot small, measure against a baseline, and only expand if the numbers are there." |
